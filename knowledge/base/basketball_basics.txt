Basketball Shooting Fundamentals

The BEEF principle is fundamental to proper shooting form:
- Balance: Keep your feet shoulder-width apart, slightly staggered
- Eyes: Focus on the back of the rim throughout the shot
- Elbow: Keep your shooting elbow under the ball, aligned with the basket
- Follow-through: Snap your wrist and hold your follow-through ("reach into the cookie jar")

Free Throw Routine
A consistent free throw routine helps players develop muscle memory:
1. Take a deep breath at the line
2. Bounce the ball 2-3 times (same number each time)
3. Find the nail on the free throw line with your dominant foot
4. Bend your knees and shoot in one fluid motion
5. Hold your follow-through until the ball hits the rim

Layup Technique
For a right-handed layup:
- Drive from the right side
- Plant your left foot
- Jump off your left foot
- Extend with your right hand
- Use the backboard, aiming for the top corner of the square
- Finish softly with a gentle touch

Three-Point Shooting Tips
- Start close and work your way out
- Power comes from the legs, not the arms
- Catch and shoot in one motion
- Practice off screens and catch-and-shoot situations
- Film yourself to identify form inconsistencies

Defensive Fundamentals
- Stay low in an athletic stance
- Keep your hands active
- Slide your feet, don't cross them
- Maintain a hand's length distance from the offensive player
- Always see both your man and the ball

Youth Development Guidelines (Ages 8-12)
- Focus on fundamentals over winning
- Keep practices fun with games and competitions
- Teach all positions - don't specialize early
- Emphasize teamwork and sportsmanship
- Use smaller balls and lower baskets when appropriate
- Short attention spans: change activities every 10-15 minutes
